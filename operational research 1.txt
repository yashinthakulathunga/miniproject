Here is the full course criteria for **MAT 2306 - Operations Research I**, followed by a short action plan.

##  MAT 2306 - Operations Research I Criteria

This course is focused on using mathematical techniques to optimize decision-making and resource allocation.

### I. Introduction and Foundations
* Introduction to Operations Research (OR): Understand the history, scope, and process of OR.
* OR Models: Differentiate between Probabilistic and Deterministic Operations Research models.

---

### II. Convex Analysis (Theoretical Basis)
* Sets and Combinations: Master the concepts of Convex combinations and Convex sets.
* Geometry: Understand Extreme points of a convex set, Convex polyhedron, Hyper planes, Half-spaces, and polytopes.
* Functions: Define and analyze Convex functions.

---

### III. Linear Programming (LP)
* Formulation: Be able to perform the Mathematical formulation of the LP problem (Objective function, constraints, non-negativity).
* Two-Dimensional LP: Solve the LP problem in two-dimensional space using Graphical solution methods.
* General LP: Understand the structure of the General LP problem.

---

### IV. The Simplex Method (Core Solution Technique)
* Algorithms: Master the standard Simplex algorithm.
* Variations: Apply the Two-phase simplex algorithm and the Revised simplex algorithm.
* Special Variables: Handle LP problems with unrestricted variables and LP problems with bounded variables.

---

### V. Duality and Special Problems
* Duality Theory: Understand Duality in LP problems and the Duality theorems.
* Dual Simplex: Apply the Dual simplex algorithm.
* Applications: Master the formulation and solution of Special types of LP problems: the Transportation problem and the Assignment problem.

---

### VI. Computer Practical
* Software: Gain an Introduction to optimization software and learn to use Excel Solver in problem solving.

##  Action Plan: Short Description to Follow
1.  Formulation: Practice converting real-world scenarios into Mathematical formulation of LP problems.
2.  Simplex Mastery: Practice all variants of the Simplex algorithm (Standard, Two-phase, Revised) until proficient in algebraic solution.
3.  Duality Concept: Understand and apply Duality theorems (e.g. strong duality, complementary slackness).
4.  Special Cases: Learn the unique structures and solution methods for the Transportation and Assignment problems.
5.  Software: Know how to use Excel Solver to verify your hand-calculated solutions.
